Pollution prevention

Pollution prevention measures reduce exposure to hazardous substances and pollutants, thereby protecting human health from respiratory diseases, waterborne illnesses, and other adverse health effects associated with pollution.

  1. Different types of pollution (air, water, soil) and their sources.
    • Air pollution: Industrial emissions, vehicle exhaust, agricultural activities, combustion of fossil fuels, wildfires, and indoor sources such as cooking and heating with solid fuels.
    • Soil pollution: Industrial activities (landfills, mining, manufacturing), agricultural practices (use of pesticides, fertilizers), improper waste disposal, and contamination from hazardous substances (heavy metals, pesticides, petroleum products).
  2. Strategies to prevent pollution and mitigate its impact on the environment and human health
    • Constructing wastewater treatment plants and sewage systems to treat and disinfect contaminated water before discharge into water bodies.
    • Promoting behavior change and responsible consumer choices to reduce waste generation, conserve resources, and minimize pollution.
